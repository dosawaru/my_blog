My Doctor of Behavioral Health Journey

The Doctor of Behavioral Health (DBH) program at Cummings Graduate Institute prepares leaders in integrated care. I began this program to gain the skills and knowledge needed to bridge the gap between physical and behavioral health systems and to manage the current fragmentation in care delivery. The DBH program emphasizes the critical connection between mental and physical health. It equips clinicians to provide patient-centered, evidence-based services across both primary and specialty care settings. Graduates are trained to drive transformation and enhance the delivery of integrated behavioral healthcare.

Three Key Skills I Will Gain

Integrated Care Delivery

I will learn to triage, consult, and deliver brief, evidence-based interventions within medical workflows. I will apply my knowledge of psychopharmacology, integrative psychiatry, and substance use disorders to support collaborative practice, safer prescribing, co-occurring care, and relapse prevention.

Population Health and Outcomes Management

I will learn to segment patient panels, manage registries, track outcomes, and design, implement, and evaluate quality improvement initiatives. I will lead cross-disciplinary teams in using data to drive action and improve care.

Leadership and Healthcare Innovation

I will develop skills in service redesign, program development, and change management, along with a deep understanding of finance and reimbursement mechanisms that support value-based care. I will use practical tools and technology to modernize care delivery.

Value to Patients

  • Faster access and reduced wait times, with proactive monitoring that prevents crises and minimizes emergency visits.
  • Safer, more coordinated care that addresses medical, behavioral, and social needs, with clear outcome metrics.
  • Whole-person care that integrates medication management, skills training, and lifestyle support to promote adherence and self-management.

Value to Colleagues

  • Real-time consultations and warm handoffs that eliminate bottlenecks, along with concise tools and patient education to improve visit efficiency.
  • Shared dashboards and brief, standardized protocols; defined roles, and unified care plans that reduce duplication.
  • Embedded behavioral health providers in medical teams, with applied project work that translates into practical clinic tools.

Value to the Community

  • Expanded access to timely behavioral health services in primary care, supported by strong referral networks with hospitals and community partners.
  • Increased capacity to serve high-risk groups, including those with substance use and dual diagnoses, using population data for targeted outreach.
  • Reduced stigma through routine integration of behavioral health services and continuous, data-driven improvements focused on high-need populations.

Value to the Organization

  • Reduced total cost of care through fewer readmissions and avoidable utilization, supported by strong ROI and scalable program models.
  • Improved performance on value-based contracts through reliable workflows, outcome tracking, and standardized quality measures.
  • New reimbursable services and streamlined documentation that protect revenue, supported by dashboards and ongoing quality improvement cycles.

The DBH program is strengthening my ability to deliver effective integrated behavioral care. This model of care will ensure patients and families benefit from early screening and timely care that is culturally responsive, trauma-informed, and stigma-free, delivered in a collaborative environment (Compendium of Behavioral Health Integration Resources for Physician Practices, 2024).
